Jermey Garnet's most-self-cited literary achievement, "The Manœuvres of Arthur" was intentionally used with the original source's ligature for the "oe" in order to enhance the comedy surrounding the high esteem he gives this "serious" work.

The phrase "a propos de bottes" kept the separation between the two words "a propos" because this is a French phrase and not the English word "apropos"
